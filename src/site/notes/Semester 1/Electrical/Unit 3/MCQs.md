## Star Connection

### Question 1
In a balanced star connected three phase system, phase voltage is _______ times line voltage.
*   A) 3
*   B) 1/3
*   C) 1.732
*   D) 0.577

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: D) 0.577**
> 
> *$V_{ph} = V_L / \sqrt{3} = V_L \times 0.577$*
</details>

### Question 2
In a balanced star connected three phase system, if phase voltage is 100V, then the line voltage is _________
*   A) 300V
*   B) 173.2V
*   C) 57.7V
*   D) 33.33V

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: B) 173.2V**
> 
> *$V_L = \sqrt{3} \times V_{ph} = 1.732 \times 100 = 173.2V$*
</details>

### Question 3
In a balanced star connected three phase system, line voltage leads phase voltage by 30 degrees.
*   A) True
*   B) False

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) True**
</details>

## Delta Connection

### Question 1
In a balanced delta connected three phase system, line current is _______ times the phase current.
*   A) 3
*   B) 1.732
*   C) 0.577
*   D) 0.333

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: B) 1.732**
> 
> *$I_L = \sqrt{3} \times I_{ph}$*
</details>

### Question 2
The line current in a balanced delta connected three phase system is 10A. The phase current is ______
*   A) 30A
*   B) 17.32A
*   C) 5.77A
*   D) 3.33A

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) 5.77A**
> 
> *$I_{ph} = I_L / \sqrt{3} = 10 / 1.732 = 5.77A$*
</details>

## Two Wattmeter Method

### Question 1
If one of the two wattmeters reads zero, the power factor of the load is ______
*   A) 1
*   B) 0.866
*   C) 0.5
*   D) < 0.5

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) 0.5**
> 
> *When $W_2 = 0$, $\tan\phi = \sqrt{3} \frac{W_1 - W_2}{W_1 + W_2} = \sqrt{3}$, so $\phi = 60°$, $\cos\phi = 0.5$.*
</details>

### Question 2
If both the wattmeters read equal value, the power factor of the load is ________
*   A) 1
*   B) 0.866
*   C) 0.5
*   D) < 0.5

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) 1**
> 
> *When $W_1 = W_2$, $\tan\phi = 0$, so $\phi = 0°$, $\cos\phi = 1$ (unity power factor).*
</details>

## Protective Devices

### Question 1
A fuse operates on the principle of
*   A) Magnetic field
*   B) Chemical reaction
*   C) Thermal heating
*   D) Arc interruption

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) Thermal heating**
> 
> *Overcurrent causes I²R heating, melting the fuse wire.*
</details>

### Question 2
Fuse wire should have:
*   A) High resistance and low melting point
*   B) Low resistance and high melting point
*   C) Low resistance and low melting point
*   D) High resistance and high melting point

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) Low resistance and low melting point**
> 
> *Low resistance minimizes power loss during normal operation; low melting point ensures quick operation during faults.*
</details>

*   D) Electrical Leakage Circuit Breaker

## Energy Calculation

### Question 1
The energy consumed by a 1KW heater run for 2 hours would be
*   A) 1 unit
*   B) 2 units
*   C) 0.5 units
*   D) 4 units

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: B) 2 units**
> 
> *Energy = Power × Time = 1 kW × 2 h = 2 kWh = 2 units.*
</details>
