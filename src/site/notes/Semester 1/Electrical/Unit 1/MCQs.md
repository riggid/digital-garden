# Unit 1: DC Circuits - MCQs

## Ohm's Law & Kirchhoff's Laws

### Question 1
Ohm's law states that V = I × R. What does R represent?
*   A) Current
*   B) Resistance
*   C) Voltage
*   D) Power

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: B) Resistance**
</details>

### Question 2
According to Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL), the algebraic sum of currents at a node is:
*   A) Maximum
*   B) Minimum
*   C) Zero
*   D) Infinite

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) Zero**
> 
> *Sum of currents entering a node equals sum of currents leaving.*
</details>

## Series & Parallel Circuits

### Question 1
In a series circuit, the total resistance is:
*   A) R₁ + R₂ + R₃
*   B) 1/(1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃)
*   C) R₁ × R₂ × R₃
*   D) R₁/R₂

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) R₁ + R₂ + R₃**
</details>

### Question 2
In a parallel circuit, the total resistance is:
*   A) R₁ + R₂ + R₃
*   B) 1/(1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃)
*   C) R₁ × R₂ × R₃
*   D) Infinite

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: B) 1/(1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃)**
</details>

## Power & Energy

### Question 1
The unit of electrical power is:
*   A) Volt
*   B) Ampere
*   C) Watt
*   D) Ohm

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) Watt**
</details>

### Question 2
Power dissipated in a resistor is given by:
*   A) P = V × I
*   B) P = I² × R
*   C) P = V²/R
*   D) All of the above

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: D) All of the above**
</details>
