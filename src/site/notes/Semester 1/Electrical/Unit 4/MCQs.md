## Induction Motors

### Question 1
The working principle of a 3-phase induction motor is based on
*   A) Lenz's law
*   B) Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction
*   C) Production of rotating magnetic field
*   D) Fleming's right-hand rule

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) Production of rotating magnetic field**
</details>

### Question 2
If a 4-pole 50 Hz 3-phase induction motor runs at 1440 rpm, then value of slip is
*   A) 0.04%
*   B) 3.3%
*   C) 4%
*   D) 6%

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) 4%**
> 
> *Synchronous speed $N_s = 120f/P = 120 \times 50/4 = 1500$ rpm.*
> *Slip $s = (N_s - N)/N_s = (1500 - 1440)/1500 = 0.04 = 4\%$.*
</details>

### Question 3
When the rotor of three phase induction motor runs at synchronous speed, the value of slip will be
*   A) 0.5
*   B) 0.3
*   C) 0
*   D) 0.4

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) 0**
> 
> *At synchronous speed, $N = N_s$, so $s = 0$.*
</details>

### Question 4
The rotor of an induction motor cannot run with synchronous speed because
*   A) Rotor torque would then become zero
*   B) Lenz's law would be violated
*   C) Induction motor would then become synchronous motor
*   D) Air friction prevents it to do so

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) Rotor torque would then become zero**
> 
> *At synchronous speed, relative motion = 0, no induced EMF, no current, no torque.*
</details>
