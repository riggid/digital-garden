# Unit 3: Quantum Mechanics & Magnetic Materials - MCQs

## Classical Free Electron Theory

### Question 1
At absolute temperature T Kelvin, the root mean square velocity of an electron of mass 'm' with 'k_B' being the Boltzmann constant is given by
*   A) $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3k_BT}{m}}$
*   B) $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{k_BT}{m}}$
*   C) $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{2k_BT}{m}}$
*   D) $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{k_BT}{2m}}$

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3k_BT}{m}}$**
> 
> *From kinetic theory: $\frac{1}{2}m v_{rms}^2 = \frac{3}{2}k_BT$*
</details>

### Question 2
According to the classical free electron theory the distribution of energy and velocity is assumed to follow
*   A) Fermi-Dirac statistics
*   B) Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics
*   C) Bose Einstein statistics
*   D) Maxwell-Dirac statistics

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: B) Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics**
</details>

## Quantum Free Electron Theory

### Question 1
According to quantum free electron theory, electrons distributed in various energy levels obey
*   A) Pauli's exclusion principle
*   B) Sommerfeld's principle
*   C) Heisenberg's uncertainty principle
*   D) D'Alembert's principle

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) Pauli's exclusion principle**
</details>

### Question 2
Particles with half-integral spin are called
*   A) Bosons
*   B) Fermions
*   C) Mesons
*   D) Photon

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: B) Fermions**
> 
> *Spin = ½, 3/2, 5/2... obey Fermi-Dirac statistics.*
</details>

### Question 3
The value of Fermi distribution at absolute zero is 1 under the condition
*   A) $E = E_F$
*   B) $E > E_F$
*   C) $E < E_F$
*   D) $E + E_F$

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: C) $E < E_F$**
> 
> *At T=0K, all states below Fermi level are filled (f=1).*
</details>

## Density of States & Fermi Energy

### Question 1
The density of states plotted against the energy gives a parabola. The area under the curve represents the
*   A) Total number of electrons in the metal
*   B) Total energy of all the electrons in the metal
*   C) The kinetic energy of electrons in the metal
*   D) The Fermi energy of the metal

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) Total number of electrons in the metal**
> 
> *$N = \int_0^{E_F} g(E) dE$*
</details>

### Question 2
The occupancy of the energy states is given by_______
*   A) $N(E) = g(E) \times f_d$
*   B) $N(E) = g(E) \times E_F$
*   C) $N(E) = g(E) \times E$
*   D) $N(E) = g(E) \times E_F \times f_d$

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) $N(E) = g(E) \times f_d$**
> 
> *Where $f_d$ is the Fermi-Dirac distribution function.*
</details>

## Para magnetism - Langevin Theory

### Question 1
The Langevin function for classical paramagnetism is given by
*   A) $L(x) = \coth(x) - \frac{1}{x}$
*   B) $L(x) = \coth(x) + \frac{1}{x}$
*   C) $L(x) = \tanh(x) - \frac{1}{x}$
*   D) $L(x) = \tanh(x) + \frac{1}{x}$

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) $L(x) = \coth(x) - \frac{1}{x}$**
> 
> *Where $x = \frac{\mu B}{k_BT}$*
</details>

### Question 2
The magnetic susceptibility of a paramagnetic material is given by
*   A) $\chi = \frac{C}{T}$ (Curie's law)
*   B) $\chi = \frac{C}{T-T_c}$ (Curie-Weiss law)
*   C) $\chi = constant$
*   D) $\chi = C \cdot T$

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) $\chi = \frac{C}{T}$ (Curie's law)**
</details>

## Spin Magnetic Moment

### Question 1
The spin magnetic moment of an electron is given by
*   A) $\mu_s = g_s \sqrt{s(s+1)} \mu_B$
*   B) $\mu_s = \sqrt{s(s+1)} \mu_B$
*   C) $\mu_s = s \mu_B$
*   D) $\mu_s = 2s \mu_B$

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) $\mu_s = g_s \sqrt{s(s+1)} \mu_B$**
> 
> *Where $g_s \approx 2$ (gyromagnetic ratio) and $s = \frac{1}{2}$.*
</details>

## Superconductivity

### Question 1
The BCS theory of superconductivity explains
*   A) Formation of Cooper pairs
*   B) Meissner effect
*   C) Zero resistance
*   D) All of the above

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: D) All of the above**
</details>

### Question 2
The Meissner effect in superconductors refers to
*   A) Complete expulsion of magnetic field
*   B) Perfect conductivity
*   C) Zero specific heat
*   D) Infinite permeability

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) Complete expulsion of magnetic field**
> 
> *Superconductors are perfect diamagnets ($B = 0$ inside).*
</details>

## Ferromagnetism

### Question 1
The Curie-Weiss law for ferromagnetic materials is
*   A) $\chi = \frac{C}{T - T_c}$
*   B) $\chi = \frac{C}{T}$
*   C) $\chi = \frac{C}{T + T_c}$
*   D) $\chi = C(T - T_c)$

<details>
<summary><strong>Check Answer</strong></summary>

> **Correct Answer: A) $\chi = \frac{C}{T - T_c}$**
> 
> *Where $T_c$ is the Curie temperature. Above $T_c$, ferromagnet → paramagnet.*
</details>
